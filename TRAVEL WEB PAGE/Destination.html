2. Pangong Tso (Pangong Lake)

1. Location: Situated at around 4,350 meters in eastern Ladakh.

2.Highlights: A high-altitude saltwater lake known for its surreal blue waters that change shades from blue to green to grey depending on the time of day. A part of the lake extends into China.

3. Best Time to Visit: May to September.

4. Unique Experience: Camping by the lakeside, stargazing at night, and visiting during sunrise or sunset for stunning reflections.

3. Nubra Valley

1. Location: About 120 km north of Leh via Khardung La Pass.

2.Highlights: Known as the "Valley of Flowers," Nubra offers lush greenery, sand dunes, and unique Bactrian camel rides.

3.Main Attractions: Diskit Monastery with a large Buddha statue, Hunder sand dunes, Panamik hot springs, and Yarab Tso (a hidden lake).

4.Best Time to Visit: June to September.

5. Thiksey Monastery

1. Location: Around 19 km east of Leh.

2. Highlights: A stunning 12-story monastery resembling the Potala Palace in Tibet, belonging to the Gelug sect of Tibetan Buddhism.

3. Features: Houses a 15-meter tall Maitreya Buddha statue, various chapels, prayer halls, and ancient manuscripts.

4. Cultural Significance: Hosts traditional mask dance festivals and daily morning prayers with monks chanting.

6. Shanti Stupa

1.Location: Perched on a hilltop in Chanspa, Leh.

2. Highlights: A white-domed stupa built to promote world peace and commemorate 2,500 years of Buddhism.

3. Viewpoint: Offers panoramic views of Leh city, especially breathtaking during sunrise and sunset.

4. Accessibility: Can be reached by climbing ~500 steps or by road.
